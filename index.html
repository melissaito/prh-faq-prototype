Assisted Living

View Service Page
Does Medicare pay for assisted living?
No. Medicare does not cover assisted living costs. It only covers medical services (doctor visits, hospital stays, limited skilled nursing). Assisted living is typically paid out-of-pocket or through long-term care insurance.
What is a senior living community?
A residential environment for older adults offering housing, meals, social activities, and varying care levels (independent living, assisted living, memory care).
Who can move into a senior living community?
Typically adults 55+ who want a supportive living environment. Residents may be independent or require assistance.
What assisted living locations are available through PruittHealth?
PruittHealth offers multiple communities, including PruittPlace locations. A location finder helps users explore options.

Skilled Nursing

View Service Page
Does Medicaid cover skilled nursing facilities?
Yes. Medicaid covers most nursing home costs for eligible individuals. Coverage varies by state and requires a certified facility.
Does Medicare cover skilled nursing facilities?

Yes, under Part A (up to 100 days per benefit period):

  • Days 1–20: Fully covered
  • Days 21–100: Covered except co-insurance

Eligibility requires:

  • 3-day hospital stay
  • Admission within 30 days
  • Available Part A days
  • Covered diagnosis
Is a skilled nursing facility a nursing home?
Yes. These facilities provide 24/7 medical care and rehabilitation for short- and long-term needs.
How much does a skilled nursing facility cost?

Varies by payment type:

  • Medicaid: Covers most costs (if eligible)
  • Medicare: Short-term coverage
  • Private Pay: Out-of-pocket
  • Medigap: Covers gaps in Medicare
  • Long-term care insurance: Policy dependent
Do skilled nursing facilities have doctors?
Yes. Care is coordinated with physicians, and staff follow personalized care plans including services, frequency, and medical needs.

Personal Care

View Service Page
Does Medicare cover in-home personal care?
No. Medicare does not cover non-medical care (bathing, dressing, meals). It may cover medical home care under specific conditions.
What is respite care?
Temporary care that gives caregivers a break. Can occur at home or in a facility and includes daily assistance and medical support.

Home Health

View Service Page
Does Medicare cover home health care?

Yes, if:

  • A doctor certifies need
  • Patient is homebound
  • Skilled services are required

Covers nursing, therapy, and supplies (not 24/7 care).

Who qualifies for home health services?
  • Under physician care
  • Needs skilled services
  • Homebound
  • Has qualifying insurance
What do home health nurses do?
  • Administer medications
  • Provide wound care
  • Monitor health
  • Educate patients/families
  • Coordinate with providers
Will Medicaid pay for home health care?
Yes, depending on state eligibility and coverage rules.

Hospice & Palliative Care

View Service Page
Does Medicare pay for hospice?
Yes, under Part A for patients with a life expectancy of 6 months or less. Covers comfort care, medications, and support services.
What does hospice do?

Focuses on comfort and quality of life:

  • Pain management
  • Emotional/spiritual support
  • Daily care assistance
  • Family counseling
Can someone choose hospice themselves?
Yes, if a doctor certifies eligibility.
Is palliative care the same as hospice?

No:

  • Palliative care: Any stage, can include treatment
  • Hospice: End-of-life care only
